Cyber AI Agents & Models

Our company builds AI agents for cybersecurity powered by a domain‑specialized model compatible with llama.cpp, Ollama, and LM Studio for efficient on‑prem and edge inference. These agents inherit deep coverage of NIST CSF 2.0, SP 800‑53, Zero Trust (SP 800‑207), RMF, and FIPS/PQC from training on 530k+ examples across 596 NIST publications, enabling policy‑aware reasoning, control mapping, and standards‑aligned guidance directly inside security workflows. Deployed as vertical software components, they integrate with CTI platforms using STIX 2.1/TAXII and stream APIs to enrich indicators, TTPs, and knowledge graphs—accelerating triage and threat investigation while preserving traceability to sources. Our GGUF‑based quantizations (Q4_K_M/Q5_K_M/Q8_0) balance speed and quality on CPU‑first infrastructure, keeping real‑time analysis feasible without dedicated GPUs. By aligning detection, risk management, and automated response with authoritative NIST guidance and modern TIPs, our agents elevate SIEM and CTI outcomes from raw alerts to actionable, standards‑conformant decisions—supporting security and resilience for critical operations.

INTEGRATIONS

Wazuh + OpenCTI/MISP — Native Integrations for Actionable Intelligence

Our agents plug into Wazuh, the open‑source unified XDR/SIEM, to enhance threat hunting through its built‑in MITRE ATT&CK module, mapping alerts to tactics and techniques and leveraging Wazuh’s file integrity monitoring, configuration assessment, and automated responses. This alignment provides immediate adversary‑behavior context and faster containment when signals surface on endpoints or servers. For OpenCTI, we support stream and enrichment connectors so that new indicators and entities create sightings against Wazuh telemetry and remain linked within OpenCTI’s STIX 2.1 knowledge graph. Where appropriate, we use the opencti‑wazuh connector to look up Wazuh events and generate correlated sightings in OpenCTI, while also enabling downstream delivery to SIEMs via OpenCTI’s live streams (e.g., Splunk/Elastic) for continuous indicator matching. For MISP, we support STIX/TAXII ingestion and the newer TAXII push flow so curated events, tags, and attributes feed detection at scale; we also leverage Wazuh’s integration patterns to check hashes/IOCs from MISP during real‑time alerting. This bi‑directional loop keeps detections current and enriches cases with provenance and confidence metadata.
